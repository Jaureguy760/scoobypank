Single-cell sequence-to-function modeling of pancreas and immune cells for large-scale variant effect prediction in type 1 diabetes

Overview

We adapt single-cell sequence-to-function (S2F) deep-learning models to predict cell-type-specific regulatory effects of genetic variants in pancreas and immune cells, enabling large-scale variant effect prediction (VEP) for type 1 diabetes (T1D).

  • T1D afflicts ~10 million people globally, with onset typically in childhood; pathogenesis is driven by both genetics and environmental factors.[B1, B2]
  • T1D is characterized by T-cell-mediated destruction of pancreatic β-cells.[B2]
  • T1D GWAS maps 165 independent risk signals (N = 817,718), and >90% of risk variants are non-coding — so the path from genetic association to biological mechanism is hard.[1]
  • Our approach: Borzoi + scooby S2F models, fine-tuned on a 733k-cell islet + immune atlas, predict cell-state-resolved variant effects and benchmark them against observed eQTLs and rare-variant signals.[2, 3, 4, 5]

Methods (high-level)

A conceptual summary of the pipeline as presented on the poster. Detailed parameters, configurations, and training code are not included here (work in progress, pre-publication).

  • Sequence-to-function backbone. Borzoi[2] predicts multi-omic coverage (RNA-seq, ATAC) from ~500 kb of DNA sequence.
  • Single-cell extension. scooby[3] equips Borzoi with a cell-conditioned decoder and LoRA fine-tuning to predict single-cell RNA & ATAC profiles from sequence, conditioned on a cell's position in a learned embedding.
  • Reference atlas. A MultiVI[5]-integrated atlas of ~733k cells across 168 donors and 5 cohorts defines shared islet + immune cell states used to condition the model.
  • Variant effect prediction. Predicted effects are benchmarked against observed single-cell eQTLs (OneK1K[4]) and bulk-islet QTLs, and applied to rare variants to surface cell-type-specific effects.
